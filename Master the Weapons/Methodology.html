Tactical Methodology

Bridging Physical Performance and Fight IQ

1. The Three IQ Pillars

Every weapon in your arsenal is measured by three mental markers. We don't just ask if you know the move, we ask if you have the IQ to apply it under fire.

Timing (T) The ability to execute the move at the exact micro-second an opening appears.
Adaptability (A) The capacity to change the move mid-execution if the opponent counters or shifts rhythm.
Energy (E) Efficiency and economy. Executing with professional poise without burning gas.

2. The Scoring Matrix

Score each weapon on a scale of 1 to 10 based on live sparring or recent fight footage:

  • 1–3 (Low IQ): Panic-based usage, poor timing, or high energy waste.
  • 4–7 (Average IQ): Technical proficiency, but struggles with rhythm changes.
  • 8–10 (Elite IQ): Professional level; the weapon is used as a trap or setup.

The "Weak Link" Rule

Your Evolution Phase is defined by your lowest-scoring weapon. If you are an elite striker (Score: 9) but your Sprawl is a "3," you are a Phase 1 fighter. We train the weakness until it matches the strength.

4. Interpreting the Visual IQ Shape

The Radar Chart provides an instant blueprint of your combat soul:

  • Symmetry: Indicates a "Generalist" with balanced skills.
  • Spikes: Your "Specialist" zones where you are most dangerous.
  • Dips: Your Critical Vulnerabilities. These are the leaks that lead to defeat.

🎯 PRO TIP: Best Video Selection Strategy

For the most accurate analysis, always use these two videos:

  • 1. Most Recent Fight – Shows current form, recent improvements or declines
  • 2. Best Performance Fight – Shows the fighter’s peak potential and true capability

This combination gives the AI the best picture of both current state and ceiling.

For Single Fighter Training (Enhanced Training module): Use the same rule — recent fight + best performance fight. This allows the system to clearly identify weaknesses that still need work and track real progress over time.

Enhanced Betting Intelligence & Recommendation Engine

The Master of Weapons Betting Matrix has been upgraded with a dual-layer analytical system to deliver sharper, more realistic tactical recommendations.

Core Philosophy

Weapon Scouting (70-80% weight) remains the foundation of the model. This is pure fight tape analysis — Timing, Adaptability, and Energy across 17 specific weapons. This tells us what each fighter is capable of technically.

The X-Factor Layer (20-30% weight)

No amount of technical scouting can fully predict real fight night performance. The new X-Factor is a contextual multiplier that captures the human and situational elements that often decide fights:

  • Aero-Cardio – Gas tank, pace recovery, and deep waters performance
  • Durability/Chin – Ability to absorb damage and stay composed
  • Psychology/Momentum – Confidence, winning/losing streaks, mental state
  • Geography/Spot – Home advantage, crowd pressure, travel, altitude
  • Corner/Technical Plan – Coach quality and in-fight adjustments
  • Physical Delta – Age, reach, height, and athletic prime adjusted

Each category is scored 0-10 per fighter. The X-Factor acts as a smart adjustment rather than overriding the tape study.

Final Scoring Formula

Final Score = (Weapon Average × 0.75) + (X-Factor Average × 0.25)

The weighting is adjustable via slider (10% – 40%) so you can tune the model based on fight style or your own edge.
